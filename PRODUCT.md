# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Busy SMB owners (5–50 employees) — restaurant owners, clinic managers, agency founders. They work long hours, run on instinct, and don't realize their business is quietly leaking money that AI could recover. Not technical. No interest in learning tools. Time-poor and skeptical of anything that sounds like "another thing to figure out."

They are not looking for AI. They are looking for relief.

## Product Purpose

BizMap maps a business's processes in ~15 minutes via a short questionnaire and surfaces exactly where time and money are leaking — then shows which AI actions would fix it. Replaces expensive generalist consultants with a system that actually knows the specific business. Free to start, no credit card.

Success looks like: a business owner finishes the map and says "I didn't know it was this simple to see."

## Brand Personality

Sharp. Direct. Confident.

Voice: speaks plainly to someone who has no patience for fluff. No jargon, no hedging, no "leverage synergies." Says the uncomfortable thing directly. Earns trust through clarity, not warmth.

Emotional goal: confidence and relief — the feeling of finally seeing the full picture.

## Anti-references

- Every generic AI SaaS landing page: floating orbs, glowing gradient text, particle backgrounds, purple-to-blue everything, "powered by AI" hero with a robot
- Sci-fi / crypto / web3 aesthetics: neon on black, circuit board patterns, synthwave palettes
- Corporate consulting: navy + gold, stock photo handshakes, hollow mission statements
- Overly warm / approachable SaaS: cream backgrounds, pastel gradients, rounded bubbly UI that looks like a to-do app

## Design Principles

1. **Earn the next sentence.** Every line of copy and every UI element must pull the eye forward. Nothing decorative earns its space.
2. **Dark but editorial, not cosmic.** Dark theme stays — but it reads like Notion's dark mode: structured, typographic, grounded — not a space screensaver.
3. **Confidence through restraint.** Don't oversell with visual noise. A direct statement in clean type is more authoritative than a glowing card.
4. **Show the problem before the solution.** The landing page works by making the user feel recognized first. Design should amplify that sequence, not rush to the CTA.
5. **Non-tech people first.** Every visual decision should be legible and unintimidating to someone who uses WhatsApp and spreadsheets, not Figma and Slack.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Hebrew RTL must be fully supported alongside English LTR — layout, spacing, and typographic rhythm must hold in both directions. No motion that cannot be disabled (respect prefers-reduced-motion). High contrast text against dark surfaces throughout.
